# Coffee Chat Complete SOP

Complete procedure for coffee chat: from reaching out to long-term follow-up.

## Quick Reference Timeline

| Phase | Timeline | Key Action |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Connect | Day 1-2 | Clear message + state intent |
| Prep | Day 3-5 | Research + prepare 10+ questions |
| Setup | Day -2 | Send Zoom link + Calendar invite |
| Morning Check | Day 0 | Morning message + confirm time |
| Execute | Chat day | On time + listen + take notes |
| Follow-up | Day +1 | Thank you email/message |
| Maintain | Week 2-4 | Share relevant content, update progress |

---

## Phase 1: Connection & Initial Request

### Message Structure

Your initial message should include:
1. **Connection point**: Why you're reaching out to them specifically
2. **Your goal**: What field/role you're exploring
3. **Specific ask**: 20 minutes for a chat, with time frame

See `templates.md` for specific templates.

### Resume Attachment Rules

**DO attach when:**
- Reaching out to HR/Hiring Manager
- Referring back after they've already seen your profile
- You've chatted multiple times and built relationship

**DON'T attach for:**
- Initial coffee chat requests
- First-time outreach to professionals
- Informational interviews

The goal is learning, not job-hunting (even if that's your ultimate goal).

## Phase 2: Preparation (Before the Chat)

### Research Checklist

Before your chat, complete this research:

- [ ] **Their LinkedIn**: Current role, past experience, education path, career transitions
- [ ] **Recent activity**: Posts, comments, articles they've shared
- [ ] **Their company**: Recent news, business model, current hiring
- [ ] **Industry trends**: What's happening in their field, competitors, challenges

### Prepare Questions (10+ Questions)

Always prepare MORE questions than you'll use. This prevents awkward silences and allows you to pick the most relevant ones during conversation.

**Career Path Questions:**
- How did you enter this field/company?
- What's been the most interesting aspect of your role?
- Were there any key turning points in your career journey?
- What surprised you most about this industry/role?

**Industry & Company Questions:**
- What changes are you seeing in this industry?
- What's your company/team currently focused on?
- How has the role evolved since you started?
- What do you think the industry will look like in 2-3 years?

**Advice Questions:**
- What makes candidates stand out to you in this field?
- If someone is transitioning into this area, what's your advice?
- What skills are most critical to develop?
- What do you wish you'd known when you started?

**Opportunity Questions:**
- Is your company/team currently hiring?
- What types of roles do you think would fit someone with my background?
- Are there other people you'd recommend I talk to?
- What resources would you recommend for learning more?

### Customize Questions by Role

**For Recent Graduates (1-3 years experience):**
- How did you find your first job in this field?
- What was your main job search channel?
- What advice would you give someone just starting out?
- What skills did you develop on the job vs. in school?

**For Experienced Professionals (3+ years):**
- What trends are you seeing in the field?
- How do you see this role evolving?
- What's your company looking for in candidates?
- What separates good performers from great ones?

**For HR / Hiring Managers:**
- What's most important for this role?
- What skills do you prioritize in candidates?
- What do new hires typically struggle with at first?
- How do you evaluate candidates during interviews?

### Prepare Your 30-Second Introduction

Practice this until it's natural:

```
Hi, I'm [Name], [current role/recent graduate] from [School/Company].
I'm exploring opportunities in [Target Field].
My background is in [Key Experience], and I'm particularly interested in [Specific Area].
```

**Keep it:**
- Under 30 seconds
- Focused on relevant background
- Clear about your goal
- Natural, not rehearsed-sounding

## Phase 3: Setup & Logistics (2 Days Before)

### Send Meeting Invite

**Critical timing**: Do this immediately after they confirm, NOT last minute.

**Steps:**
1. Create calendar event with specific date/time
2. Add their email as attendee
3. Include Zoom link in calendar event
4. Send confirmation message: "I've sent a calendar invite with the Zoom link"

**Why this matters:**
- Shows professionalism
- Reduces no-shows significantly
- Ensures they have link ahead of time
- Demonstrates you value their time

## Phase 4: Day of Chat

### Morning Check-in (8-10 AM)

Send a brief message:

```
Hi [Name],

Looking forward to our chat at [Time] today!
Here's the Zoom link: [LINK]

See you soon!
```

**Purpose**: Final reminder, reconfirms they didn't forget

### Pre-Chat Checklist (5 Minutes Before)

- [ ] Join Zoom 5 minutes early
- [ ] Test camera, microphone, internet connection
- [ ] Clean/professional background (real or virtual)
- [ ] Professional appearance (at least upper half visible)
- [ ] Silence ALL notifications (phone, computer, apps)
- [ ] Have notebook/document ready for notes
- [ ] Have your prepared questions accessible
- [ ] Glass of water nearby

### Chat Structure (20 Minutes Total)

**Opening (2-3 minutes)**
- Thank them for their time
- Brief small talk (weather, their day) - keep under 1 minute
- Your 30-second introduction
- Set context: "I'd love to learn about [specific topic]"

**Main Conversation (15-18 minutes)**
- **Listen 60%, speak 40%**
- Take notes (shows you care, captures insights)
- After each answer: summarize + ask follow-up question
- Use: "So what you're saying is..." or "Could you tell me more about..."
- Ask your prepared questions, but let conversation flow naturally
- Read the room - if they're engaged and talkative, let them talk

**Closing (3-5 minutes)**
- Thank them again for specific insights
- Ask: "Is there any final advice you'd give someone in my position?"
- Request: "Could we connect on [WeChat/Email/LinkedIn] to stay in touch?"
- Clarify: "What's the best way to follow up with you?"

## Common Errors & Solutions

| Error | Problem | Solution |
|-------|---------|----------|
| Not enough questions | Conversation dies after 10 min | Prepare 10+ questions, take notes, ask follow-ups |
| Complaining mode | Talking about stress/problems | Share goals, ask "What should I do?" not "How do I feel?" |
| Forgot to send link | They don't know how to join | Send Calendar invite + message immediately after confirmation |
| Running late | Late or no-show | Start prep 15 min early, join Zoom 5 min before |
| No follow-up | Nothing happens after | Get WeChat/email during chat, message within 24 hours |
| Wrong questions | Asking irrelevant things | Research before chat, customize questions by their role |
| Too much talking | You dominate conversation | 40% you, 60% them - they're the expert |
| No notes | Forget key insights | Take notes during chat, review immediately after |

## Phase 5: Follow-Up Strategy

### Immediate Follow-up (Within 24 Hours)

**Required actions:**
1. Send personalized thank you email/message
2. Reference specific insights they shared
3. Mention action you'll take based on their advice
4. Record key insights in your notes

**Template structure:**
```
Thank you for [specific thing]
I especially appreciated [specific insight]
I'm going to [action based on their advice]
Will keep you updated
```

### Week 2-4: Relationship Building

**Share value without asking:**
- Send relevant article about their industry
- Share news about their company
- Provide update on your progress
- Introduce them to someone in your network who could help them

**Goal**: Be helpful, not just help-seeking

### Long-term Maintenance (Every 2-3 Months)

**Keep in touch by:**
- Sharing relevant content
- Providing progress updates
- Congratulating them on achievements (LinkedIn updates)
- Making warm introductions when relevant

**Why this matters**: When opportunities arise in 3-6 months, you'll be top of mind.

## What Makes a Good Coffee Chat

### Successful Pattern:

1. Clear message explaining connection and goal
2. Brief, natural small talk (under 1 minute)
3. Thoughtful questions based on research
4. Active listening + note-taking
5. Follow-up questions showing engagement
6. Request for feedback on your approach
7. Personalized thank you within 24 hours
8. Share relevant article 2 weeks later
9. 3-6 months later: they think of you for opportunities

### Signs It Went Well:

- They talk more than you do
- Conversation goes longer than planned
- They offer to introduce you to others
- They ask about your background/goals
- They volunteer advice without prompting
- They give you their direct contact info

### Signs to Improve:

- Lots of awkward silences
- You're doing most of talking
- They seem distracted or checking time
- Conversation feels like interrogation
- They don't offer any next steps

## Core Rules (Never Break These)

1. **Be on time** - Join 5 minutes early, always
2. **Listen more than talk** - 40% you, 60% them
3. **Take notes** - Shows respect and captures insights
4. **Follow up** - Within 24 hours, every time
5. **Stay in touch** - Long-term relationships win

## Key Mindset Shifts

### This is NOT an Interview

**Don't:**
- Try to impress them with your achievements
- Ask "Will you refer me?" in first conversation
- Treat it as a job application
- Get defensive about your background

**Do:**
- Be genuinely curious about their experience
- Build authentic connection
- Learn something valuable
- Respect their time and expertise

### Long-term Over Short-term

**Bad approach:**
- Only reach out when you need something
- Disappear after getting what you want
- Focus solely on immediate job opportunities

**Good approach:**
- Build real relationships first
- Provide value when you can
- Stay in touch even when not job searching
- Think in months/years, not days/weeks

**Reality**: Most coffee chats don't lead to immediate jobs. They plant seeds. The person who helped you 6 months from now is someone you talked to today.

### Abundance Mindset

**If one chat goes poorly:**
- Learn from it
- Don't take it personally
- Move on to the next one
- Volume matters - do 10-20 coffee chats

**The math**: If you do 20 coffee chats and only 3 lead to strong connections, those 3 connections could change your career trajectory.

## Questions to Ask Yourself After Each Chat

Self-assessment checklist:

- [ ] Did I do more listening than talking?
- [ ] Did I take notes on key insights?
- [ ] Did I ask thoughtful, researched questions?
- [ ] Did I learn something genuinely valuable?
- [ ] Did I get their direct contact info?
- [ ] Did they seem engaged and interested?
- [ ] Did I follow up within 24 hours?
- [ ] What would I do differently next time?

Use this to improve each chat progressively.
